Statistical Arbitrage was v3 or v4 of a financial markets trading system. This is the one I submitted as my Bachelor's capstone. Older variants actually traded by themselves, fully hands-free, using the MetaTrader5 platform. This one is just a web dashboard that pulls data from Yahoo Finance, shows historical trades, and tells you if there's one today or not. It also plots the data it uses to compute entries and exits, but you can't see that in the screenshot.

If you've ever seen the chart of a stock before, you'll know it goes up and down a lot. It's mostly Brownian motion, but not 100%--there are some underlying patterns and relationships. For example, if you look at the chart of two related stocks side-by-side, say, Coke and Pepsi, you'll see that they look pretty much the same. Not EXACTLY the same, but really close, and that's the point. If you subtract the price of one from the other over time, it'd dance around a mean. Sometimes it'd go up, sometimes it'd go down, but over time it would converge to some mean. In other words, if you plotted a moving average with a large enough window, it'd be flat.

And there's the key idea. We know that if it moves up it'll probably come back down. And that if it moves down it'll probably come back up. And knowing what will probably happen in the future is exactly how you make money in financial markets, assuming you are correct more than you're not. Which, believe me, is much harder than it looks.

This one I wrote in about 5 minutes, but it perfectly encapsulates my worldview of software. I can't stand bloated programs, programs that try to be more than they are, programs that do stuff you didn't ask it to and you don't want it to. Maybe it's a control thing, but I like my software to conform to the UNIX philosophy: do one thing and do it well. And it seems all too common today for software to try to do everything. Facebook with its stories and marketplace and dating and reels and games and... could go on and on. And you could say the same for arguably most stuff nowadays. That drives me up a wall. It's my belief that Facebook should be a social media where you see your friends' posts in chronological order. Photoshop should be a software for editing photos. Google should be a search engine. Why does everything need "AI" nowadays? Why does everything try to be... everything? We need Web3 asap bruh.
